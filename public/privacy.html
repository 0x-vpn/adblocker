Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 5, 2026

0x VPN is operated by Unos Pvt Ltd. This policy describes the Android VPN app and related static website.

What 0x VPN does

0x VPN provides VPN connectivity. When connected, Android routes device network traffic through the selected VPN server. Users may optionally enable hosts-based DNS ad blocking for tunneled apps. Rewarded ads may be used to grant server-authorized VPN minutes.

Data we process

  • Username, account state, recovery-key authentication records, and Supabase session state.
  • Selected VPN location, entitlement status, VPN-minute balance, and connection status events.
  • Coarse country code used for country and location availability rules.
  • Rewarded ad transaction data needed to verify VPN-minute rewards.
  • App-scoped device-identity signals for fraud prevention and account-abuse review.
  • Android Keystore device-key proof and resettable installation credential for installation continuity and abuse review.
  • Google Play Integrity verdict summary when integrity checks are configured.
  • The client public IP address seen by the backend, transformed with a server-side keyed hash for passive abuse review.
  • Bounded diagnostic events with stable error codes and sanitized locations.

Data we do not store

We do not store browsing history, full URLs, DNS query logs, traffic payloads, packet contents, or full VPN traffic logs. Raw VPN profile text is not stored in client-readable tables and is not logged by the app.

Device and abuse-prevention signals

The app may derive an app-scoped device hash from Widevine MediaDRM where the device allows it, otherwise from Android's app-scoped Android ID. Raw identifiers remain on device. The backend applies a separate server-keyed HMAC before storing links. The app does not build a fingerprint from CPU layout, display metrics, or sensor inventories.

The app may also create a non-exportable Android Keystore signing key and use it to sign one-time backend challenges. The server verifies the proof and stores only a keyed public-key fingerprint. A server-issued resettable installation credential is stored encrypted in app-private storage; the server stores only its keyed hash.

Ad blocking

When Ad-block is enabled, the app checks a hosted static manifest and may download a compact hashed-domain artifact. Filtering happens locally inside the VPN tunnel. Private DNS, app-embedded encrypted DNS, direct TCP DNS, first-party ads, and browser-extension-only rules may bypass DNS filtering.

Ads

Rewarded ads are optional and user-initiated. Ad completion is verified server-side before VPN minutes are granted. Ads do not block, inject, modify, or manipulate VPN traffic.

Service providers

  • Supabase for authentication, database, Edge Functions, account, entitlement, and profile-delivery state.
  • Google AdMob for rewarded ads and server-side verification.
  • Google Play Integrity for app and device integrity verification when configured.
  • VPN infrastructure providers for selected VPN locations.
  • GitHub Pages for static website, ad-block artifacts, and optional direct update artifacts.

Account deletion

Users can request account deletion in the app. Deletion removes supported customer profile, device identity links, reward, entitlement, session, and audit data associated with the account.
